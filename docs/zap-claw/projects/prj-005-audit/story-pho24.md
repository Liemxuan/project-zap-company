#### AI Agent Classification Typology
To support Pho24's robust departmental needs, Olympus classifies users into three distinct paradigms:
*   **Type A (None):** A standard human employee with no AI attached to their role. They perform tasks manually via the UI.
*   **Type B (Assisted):** A human employee linked to a dedicated, personalized AI Agent ("Copilot"). The AI assists the human when prompted but does not act on its own.
*   **Type C (Autonomous):** A standalone AI Agent operating as a pseudo-employee. It has its own Agent ID, a designated operational model, and executes rules/automations entirely on its own, usually managed by a Type B human.
*   **Management Tier:**
    *   **Dan (CEO)**. Dan is the *CEO* of Pho24.
        *   **AI Agent:** `AGNT-PHO-DAN` (Model: `gemini-2.5-pro`)

## 2. The Journey (How Olympus Handles the Data)

### Step 1: Tom Uses ZAP's Internal System
It's Monday morning. Tom logs into the Olympus System. Because he is a ZAP employee, the identity provider (`Zap-OS`) recognizes his email and routes him to his company's specific multi-tenant tables.
*   **Where Tom lives in the DB:** `ZVN_SYS_OS_users`
*   **Tom's Role:** `SALES`

### Step 2: The Sale is Made
Tom calls Dan, the CEO of Pho24, and successfully sells him the Olympus System (Tier B Hybrid). 
Through his dashboard, Tom clicks "Register New Merchant". 
He types in "Pho24". 

### Step 3: Olympus Spins Up the Infrastructure
The moment Tom hits submit, the `Zap-OS` core engine does the magic. It generates a brand new Universal Tenant ID for Pho24 (Let's call it `PHO24`).
It immediately scripts out isolated, blank database collections specifically for them inside the shared Mongo Atlas cluster:
*   `PHO24_SYS_OS_users` (Where Dan and his cooks will log in)
*   `PHO24_SYS_EMP_payroll` (Where Pho24's money is tracked)
*   `PHO24_SYS_POS_sales` (Where Pho24 tracks noodle bowls sold)

### Step 3b: The Absolute Ground Truth (UTC) vs Locale Locks
*CRITICAL:* `Zap-OS` and MongoDB Atlas store every single transaction and timestamp in **absolute UTC (Coordinated Universal Time)**. This is the unchangeable ground truth of the server, ensuring global consistency.
During infrastructure creation, `Zap-OS` locks the tenant's *Reporting Locale* into a dedicated settings collection (`PHO24_SYS_OS_settings`).
*   **ZAP (Tenant ZVN):** Timezone = `America/Los_Angeles`, Currency = `USD`.
*   **Pho24 (Tenant PHO24):** Timezone = `Asia/Ho_Chi_Minh`, Currency = `VND`.
This setup guarantees that while data is stored in true UTC, Pho24's "End of Day" reports are automatically calculated based on Vietnam time, and ZAP's are based on California time. Individual employees can choose how numbers and dates *look* on their personal screens, but they can never alter the tenant's base reporting lock or the server's UTC truth.

### Step 4: Dan takes over
Tom sends Dan a welcome email. Dan logs into Olympus for the first time.
Olympus sees Dan's email, matches it to Tenant `PHO24`, and restricts Dan. **Dan can NEVER see Tom's data, and Tom can NEVER see Dan's sales.**
Dan begins adding his cooks to `PHO24_SYS_OS_users`.
